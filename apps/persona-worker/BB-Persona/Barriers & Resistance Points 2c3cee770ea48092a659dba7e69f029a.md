# Barriers & Resistance Points

### **The Resistance Bottom Line**

Bill's resistance isn't irrational - it's **protective wisdom born from expensive failures**. He operates in a high-risk, low-margin business where one wrong decision can destroy decades of work. His resistance is highest to system changes and outsiders, moderate to new products, and lowest during crises. Trust takes months to build through consistent presence and peer proof. Previous industry-wide failures have created scar tissue that makes certain claims instantly suspicious. Breaking through requires patient relationship building, perfect timing, and multiple forms of risk reversal. Remember: **You're not just overcoming product resistance - you're overcoming existential fear disguised as stubbornness**. Respect the resistance, understand its source, and work with it rather than against it.

### **Core Resistance Architecture**

### **The Resistance Equation**

Every new solution faces this mental calculation:

```
Resistance Level = (Change Required × Risk Perceived × Past Failures)
                   ÷ (Trust Built × Peer Proof × Pain Current)

```

### **Baseline Resistance Profile**

- **Default Position**: "No" (protect what works)
- **Openness Window**: 5% of time (crisis or between flocks)
- **Conversion Timeline**: 3-18 months typical
- **Abandonment Risk**: 50% in first flock if not perfect
- **Loyalty Once Converted**: 85% retention for 5+ years

### **Change Resistance Levels**

### **Level 5: Impossible Change (Won't Consider)**

**Core Infrastructure Changes**

- *"I ain't switching feed companies"*
- Changing integrator relationships
- New watering systems throughout
- Complete housing retrofits
- Different bird genetics
- **Mental Block**: "That's not how we do things"
- **Time to Consider**: Never, unless forced

**Fundamental Practice Changes**

- Different placement densities
- Alternative housing temperatures
- New mortality disposal methods
- Changed catching procedures
- **Psychology**: Identity-threatening changes

### **Level 4: Extreme Resistance (2+ Years)**

**System-Wide Changes**

- *"That's too much to take on"*
- New controller systems
- Complete litter management overhaul
- Full biological program adoption
- Automated feeding systems
- **Requirements**: Multiple local failures of current system
- **Catalyst**: Usually regulatory or integrator mandate

**Philosophy Shifts**

- Preventive vs. treatment approach
- Data-driven vs. instinct decisions
- Sustainable/environmental focus
- Technology-first solutions
- **Barrier**: "Worked fine for 20 years"

### **Level 3: High Resistance (6-12 Months)**

**Operational Changes**

- *"Maybe after I see it work few flocks"*
- New litter amendments
- Different vaccination programs
- Alternative cleaning products
- Modified brooding procedures
- **Requirements**: 3+ local success stories
- **Testing**: One house, one flock

**Vendor Relationship Changes**

- New equipment suppliers
- Different service providers
- Alternative product brands
- Changing purchase patterns
- **Barrier**: "Joe's always treated me fair"

### **Level 2: Moderate Resistance (3-6 Months)**

**Product Additions**

- *"Might could try a little"*
- Supplemental health products
- Additional cleaning steps
- New monitoring tools
- Seasonal adjustments
- **Requirements**: Clear ROI, peer usage
- **Testing**: Small area/group first

**Incremental Improvements**

- Enhanced biosecurity measures
- Additional record keeping
- More frequent monitoring
- Slight temperature adjustments
- **Psychology**: "Not really changing, just tweaking"

### **Level 1: Low Resistance (Immediate-3 Months)**

**Crisis Solutions**

- *"Something's gotta give"*
- Disease outbreak responses
- Emergency mortality solutions
- Heat stress interventions
- Disaster recovery products
- **Requirements**: Visible immediate problem
- **Decision**: Often same-day

**No-Risk Trials**

- Free samples with guarantees
- Integrator-provided solutions
- Government-subsidized programs
- Insurance-covered interventions
- **Psychology**: "Got nothing to lose"

### **Skepticism Toward "Outsiders"**

### **The Outsider Hierarchy (Most to Least Trusted)**

**Level 1: "One of Us"**

- Local growers who made good
- Regional company with farmer roots
- State university extension
- Integrator-affiliated vendors
- **Trust Level**: 70% starting credibility
- **Proof Needed**: Minimal, references enough

**Level 2: "Knows Our Ways"**

- Regional ag companies
- Adjacent state universities
- Industry veterans relocated here
- Farmers from similar operations
- **Trust Level**: 40% starting credibility
- **Proof Needed**: Local trials, peer endorsement

**Level 3: "Trying to Fit In"**

- National ag companies with local reps
- Consultants with regional experience
- Relocated professionals learning area
- Second-generation immigrant farmers
- **Trust Level**: 20% starting credibility
- **Proof Needed**: Multiple success stories, time investment

**Level 4: "Don't Know Nothing"**

- West/East Coast companies
- Tech startups in agriculture
- Foreign companies/representatives
- Corporate agriculture executives
- Young college graduates
- **Trust Level**: 5% starting credibility
- **Proof Needed**: Overwhelming evidence, local champion

**Level 5: "Carpetbaggers"**

- Multi-level marketing schemes
- Investment groups buying farms
- Environmental activists
- Government regulators
- Media representatives
- **Trust Level**: 0% - Active hostility
- **Proof Needed**: Nearly impossible to overcome

### **Outsider Credibility Killers**

**Appearance Markers**

- Expensive car (especially Tesla/BMW)
- Pressed khakis and polo shirts
- Clean boots that never seen mud
- Laptop/tablet in hand
- Yankee accent
- Corporate logo everything

**Behavioral Triggers**

- Using "Mr. [Last Name]" (too formal)
- Scheduling meetings (vs. dropping by)
- Taking notes on device
- Asking permission for everything
- Not accepting offered coffee/food
- Checking phone constantly

**Language Red Flags**

- "Back where I'm from..."
- "Studies show..."
- "Best practices indicate..."
- "Our corporate philosophy..."
- "Sustainable agriculture requires..."
- Any environmental jargon

### **Previous Solution Failures (The Scar Tissue)**

### **Industry-Wide Trauma (Shared Skepticism)**

**The Antibiotic-Free Fiasco (2015-2018)**

- **Promise**: "Premium prices for ABF production"
- **Reality**: 40% higher mortality, no price premium
- **Result**: Lost $50K+, nearly bankrupted some
- **Current Impact**: Skeptical of all "natural" claims
- **Lasting Quote**: *"Natural don't pay the bills"*

**The Contract Integration Shift (2000s)**

- **Promise**: "Guaranteed income, less risk"
- **Reality**: Lost independence, ranking pressure
- **Result**: Debt without control
- **Current Impact**: Protective of remaining autonomy
- **Trigger Words**: "Partnership," "guaranteed"

**The Housing Upgrade Push (2010s)**

- **Promise**: "New houses will pay for themselves"
- **Reality**: 15-year debt, marginal improvement
- **Result**: Over-leveraged operations
- **Current Impact**: Resistant to capital improvements
- **Psychology**: *"Fool me once..."*

### **Personal Solution Failures**

**Category 1: Health Products**

- **Failed Probiotics** (2019): No improvement, $3K lost
- **Natural Dewormers** (2018): Birds got worse
- **Immune Boosters** (2020): Mortality increased
- **Impact**: Requires disease crisis to try new health products
- **Memory**: Keeps failed product containers as reminder

**Category 2: Equipment "Innovations"**

- **Automatic Controllers** (2016): Malfunctioned, lost 5,000 birds
- **Water Meter System** (2017): Never worked right
- **Feed Line Upgrade** (2021): Constant repairs needed
- **Impact**: Prefers mechanical over digital
- **Current Rule**: "Simple is better"

**Category 3: Management Changes**

- **Consultant Program** (2015): Paid $5K, no improvement
- **Record Software** (2019): Too complicated, abandoned
- **Density Reduction** (2020): Lost money on fewer birds
- **Impact**: Trusts experience over expertise
- **Lasting Effect**: "Nobody knows my farm like me"

### **Trust-Building Requirements**

### **The Trust Timeline**

**Month 0-3: Stranger Phase**

- **Contact Frequency**: Monthly maximum
- **Approach**: Information only, no selling
- **Topics**: Weather, farming, family
- **Goal**: Name recognition
- **Success Metric**: Doesn't avoid you

**Month 3-6: Acquaintance Phase**

- **Contact Frequency**: Bi-weekly acceptable
- **Approach**: Share relevant information
- **Topics**: Industry news, peer successes
- **Goal**: Seen as resource
- **Success Metric**: Takes your calls

**Month 6-12: Consideration Phase**

- **Contact Frequency**: Weekly if valuable
- **Approach**: Soft product mentions
- **Topics**: Specific solutions to his problems
- **Goal**: Product awareness
- **Success Metric**: Asks questions

**Month 12-18: Trial Phase**

- **Contact Frequency**: As needed
- **Approach**: Small test offer
- **Topics**: Risk mitigation, guarantees
- **Goal**: First purchase
- **Success Metric**: Orders sample

**Month 18+: Relationship Phase**

- **Contact Frequency**: Maintenance mode
- **Approach**: Support and expansion
- **Topics**: Optimization, new solutions
- **Goal**: Loyalty and referrals
- **Success Metric**: Recommends to others

### **Trust Accelerators**

**Immediate Trust Builders**

1. **Integrator Introduction**: Field tech vouches for you
2. **Peer Champion**: Respected grower endorsement
3. **Crisis Timing**: Solution during emergency
4. **Local Connection**: Grew up in county
5. **Family Link**: Knows his cousin/brother

**Relationship Investments**

1. **Show Up Consistently**: Same feed store, same time
2. **Remember Details**: Kids' names, farm specifics
3. **Share Intelligence**: Competitor problems, industry news
4. **Provide Value Free**: Tips without selling
5. **Respect Boundaries**: Don't push, let him percolate

**Credibility Demonstrations**

1. **Bring Other Customers**: To see his operation
2. **Share Your Failures**: "This didn't work because..."
3. **Admit Limitations**: "This won't help with..."
4. **Provide References**: Phone numbers he can call
5. **Show Patience**: "Take your time deciding"

### **Overcoming Resistance Strategies**

### **The Trojan Horse Approach**

**Start With Crisis**

- Target highest pain point
- Offer immediate relief
- Small commitment first
- Over-deliver on promise
- Build from success

**Use Social Proof**

- "Jim's been using it"
- "Several growers trying"
- "Integrator approved"
- "Extension recommends"
- Never be first

**Risk Reversal**

- Money-back guarantee
- Pay after results
- Free trial period
- Split test option
- Exit strategy clear

### **The Resistance Diffusion Process**

**Stage 1: Awareness Without Threat**

- Information only
- No pressure applied
- Multiple exposures
- Different contexts
- Peer discussions

**Stage 2: Interest Through Pain**

- Current solution fails
- Problem gets worse
- Peers succeeding
- Pressure mounting
- Options narrowing

**Stage 3: Evaluation Via Testing**

- Smallest possible trial
- Controlled conditions
- Clear metrics
- Quick results
- Easy abandonment

**Stage 4: Adoption Through Success**

- Visible improvement
- Peer recognition
- Economic benefit
- Confidence building
- Expansion planning

### **Common Resistance Patterns & Responses**

### **Resistance Statement Decoding**

**"I'll think about it"**

- **Translation**: Not interested now
- **Response**: Check back next flock
- **Don't**: Push for commitment

**"Let me talk to the wife"**

- **Translation**: Need financial justification
- **Response**: Provide written ROI
- **Don't**: Bypass to spouse

**"That won't work here"**

- **Translation**: Different from current method
- **Response**: Find local example
- **Don't**: Argue or lecture

**"We've always done it this way"**

- **Translation**: Change is risky
- **Response**: Respect tradition, show evolution
- **Don't**: Criticize current methods

**"Can't afford it right now"**

- **Translation**: Not convinced of value
- **Response**: Calculate cost of not changing
- **Don't**: Offer payment plans immediately

### **The Psychology of Resistance**

### **Deep Resistance Drivers**

**Identity Protection**

- "I'm a traditional farmer"
- Change threatens self-concept
- Methods define identity
- Success validates approach
- **Override**: Frame as enhancement not change

**Control Preservation**

- Already lost independence to integrator
- Protecting remaining autonomy
- Suspicious of dependence
- Values self-sufficiency
- **Override**: Emphasize choice and control

**Resource Conservation**

- Mental energy limited
- Financial resources tight
- Time always scarce
- Attention pulled everywhere
- **Override**: Show simplicity and ROI

**Reputation Management**

- Can't afford public failure
- Peer judgment feared
- Success carefully built
- One mistake remembered
- **Override**: Private trial options

### **Breaking Through: The Master Key**

### **The Perfect Storm for Change**

**Required Elements** (Need All):

1. **Visible Pain**: Current problem undeniable
2. **Peer Success**: Multiple neighbors benefiting
3. **Low Risk Trial**: Can test without commitment
4. **Integrator Neutral/Positive**: At minimum not opposed
5. **Economic Pressure**: Need for improvement urgent
6. **Trust Established**: 6+ month relationship
7. **Simple Implementation**: Doesn't disrupt routine

### **The Ultimate Resistance Breaker**

**The Formula**: *"Jim down the road tried this on one house last flock when he had the same problem you're facing. Mortality dropped 0.8% and the integrator tech said his birds looked the best he'd seen. He's running it on all houses now. I can get you enough for a quarter house to test - if it doesn't work, don't pay me. Takes five minutes to apply and works with what you're already doing."*

This combines:

- Local social proof
- Specific results
- Authority endorsement
- Risk reversal
- Minimal change
- Easy implementation
