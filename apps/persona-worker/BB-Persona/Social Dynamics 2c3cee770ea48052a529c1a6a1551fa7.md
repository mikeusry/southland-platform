# Social Dynamics

### **The Social Bottom Line**

Bill exists in a complex social web where **cooperation and competition dance together daily**. He's a **solid middle influencer** who leads by practical example while following on innovation. His **offline relationships drive 85% of his decisions**, with online serving mainly as information gathering. He maintains multiple personas across different groups, carefully managing his social capital like a financial portfolio. Understanding these dynamics is crucial - products succeed or fail based on social proof, and Bill won't move without his network's subtle permission. He's not just buying products; he's managing a reputation that took decades to build and could be destroyed in one bad season.

### **Social Ecosystem Architecture**

### **The Concentric Circles of Trust**

**Inner Circle (3-5 people)**

- **The Coffee Crew**: 2-3 growers who know everything
- **Wife**: Business partner and emotional anchor
- **Best Friend**: Often non-farmer, childhood friend
- **Mentor**: Older grower who taught him the business
- **Trust Level**: Share actual numbers, real problems

**Middle Circle (10-15 people)**

- **Church Family**: Deacons, Sunday school class
- **Complex Growers**: Settlement comparison group
- **Family**: Brothers, cousins, in-laws
- **Service Providers**: Feed store owner, equipment tech
- **Trust Level**: Share general success/challenges

**Outer Circle (30-50 people)**

- **Community**: Rotary, school board, neighbors
- **Extended Industry**: Other integrator growers
- **Online Groups**: Facebook poultry forums
- **Casual Church**: Congregation members
- **Trust Level**: Public persona only

### **Competitive vs. Collaborative Balance**

### **The Cooperation Paradox**

*"We help each other succeed while secretly hoping to beat everyone"*

### **Collaborative Zones (80% of Interactions)**

**Information Sharing**

- **Disease Alerts**: Immediate, no hesitation
- **Weather Warnings**: "Storm coming, check your generators"
- **Equipment Problems**: "Watch out for that feed motor issue"
- **Integrator Changes**: "New field tech is strict on..."
- **Supplier Issues**: "Don't buy from X, quality problems"

**Resource Sharing**

- **Emergency Support**: Lends generators during power loss
- **Equipment**: Shares specialized tools
- **Labor**: Helps with catastrophic mortality removal
- **Knowledge**: Teaches new growers basics
- **BUT**: Never during his own crisis

**Collective Negotiation**

- Groups together for feed store discounts
- Shares trucking costs for supplies
- Informal price-fixing resistance
- United front with integrator on major issues
- Community bulk buying programs

### **Competitive Zones (20% but Emotionally 80%)**

**Settlement Rankings**

- **Public**: "We all did pretty well"
- **Private**: Studies rankings obsessively
- **Strategy**: Never shares winning formula
- **Information War**: Fishes for others' numbers without revealing
- **Mental Score**: Knows three-year running tally

**Innovation Adoption**

- Won't share new products until after settlement
- Downplays success of new methods
- Watches what top grower does secretly
- Tests innovations without announcing
- Claims "nothing special" when winning

**Resource Competition**

- Best chick placement timing
- Preferred field tech attention
- First choice on equipment service
- Premium feed delivery slots
- Integration expansion opportunities

### **Bill's Influence Tactics**

**Building Influence**

- Shares successes indirectly through wife
- Brings visual proof (photos) not just stories
- Helps new growers to build loyalty network
- Strategic humility with strategic revelations
- Consistent presence without overexposure

**Wielding Influence**

- Plants seeds: "Been thinking about trying..."
- Tests reactions before committing position
- Uses questions to guide: "Y'all ever wonder if..."
- Creates bandwagons: "Few of us are trying..."
- Never first, never last on changes

**Protecting Influence**

- Never admits major failures
- Reframes setbacks as experiments
- Maintains equipment appearance
- Keeps farm "tour-ready"
- Consistent church/community presence

- **Equipment Demos**: Social more than educational

### **Online World (15% of Social Capital)**

**Facebook Behavior**

- **Check Frequency**: 2-3x daily, 10-15 minutes
- **Posting Frequency**: 1-2x per month
- **Post Types**: Success photos, family milestones
- **Groups**: 2-3 poultry, 1 local community, 1 church
- **Engagement**: Likes > Comments > Shares > Posts

**Facebook Groups Dynamic**

- "Georgia Poultry Growers" (2,500 members)
    - Lurks daily
    - Posts only major successes
    - Comments on disease threads
- "Broiler Producers Network" (5,000 members)
    - Information gathering only
    - Never posts
    - Screenshots interesting posts
- "[County] Community Board" (800 members)
    - Wife more active
    - Responds to local issues
    - Promotes church events

**Digital Reputation Management**

- Never posts problems or complaints
- Photos carefully curated
- No political posts (likes only)
- Family photos get most engagement
- Business success subtle not boastful

**What He Never Posts**

- Actual production numbers
- Settlement sheets
- Dead birds or problems
- Complaints about integrator
- Political opinions
- Financial stress
- Family conflicts
