## **Environmental Stance**

- **Practical Steward**: Sees himself as caring for the land but prioritizes farm survival
- Skeptical of "environmental extremists" but wants to be responsible
- Believes farmers are the "original environmentalists"
- Will adopt environmental practices IF they improve performance or reduce costs
- Concerned about regulations but not activism

## **Technology Adoption Attitude**

- **Reluctant but Practical**: Late majority adopter (waits for proof)
- Trusts what other successful farmers use first
- Prefers simple, reliable over complex, cutting-edge
- "If it ain't broke, don't fix it" mentality
- Will use smartphones for texting but not apps
- Suspicious of "computer-controlled" solutions
- Values equipment he can fix himself

## **View on Organic/Natural Products**

- **Cautiously Interested**:
- Skeptical of "organic" as marketing hype
- BUT interested in "natural" if it means safer/effective
- Associates organic with expensive and unproven
- Worried natural = weaker/less effective
- More receptive to "proven biological solutions" than "organic"
- Key phrase: "I'll try anything that works, but it better work"

### **Risk Tolerance Level**

- **Risk-Averse Conservative**:
- Will try new things only on small test groups first
- Needs 2-3 other farmers' success stories before adopting
- More afraid of losing what he has than missing opportunities
- One bad flock can ruin him financially

### **Innovation Adoption Curve Position**

- **Late Majority** (waits for social proof)
- Needs to see it working for neighbors first
- Adopts when it becomes standard practice
- Won't be first or last

### **Data vs. Gut Decision Preference**

- **Gut First, Data Second**:
- Trusts experience over spreadsheets
- Likes simple metrics (mortality %, weights)
- Suspicious of complex analytics
- "I can tell by looking at my birds"

### **Authority Figures & Trust**

- **Hierarchy of Trust**:
    1. Successful local farmers (especially contract top performers)
    2. Integrator field techs (with caution)
    3. University extension (if local)
    4. Feed store recommendations
    5. Online/distant experts (least trusted)

### **Fears** (in priority order)

1. Losing integrator contract
2. Disease wiping out flock
3. Can't pay bills/bankruptcy
4. Being seen as a failure by family/community
5. Government regulations shutting him down
6. Being taken advantage of by suppliers

### **Information Consumption Habits**

- **Morning coffee** (5-6 AM): Quick check of weather, markets
- **Lunch break**: Phone calls with other farmers
- **Evening** (after 7 PM): Facebook scrolling, maybe YouTube
- Reads trade publications in bathroom/waiting rooms
- Attends winter meetings/conferences when flocks are out

### **Skepticism Triggers**

- "Too good to be true" claims
- College kids telling him how to farm
- Complicated science explanations
- West Coast/foreign companies
- Anything that sounds like "environmental activism"
- Products that require changing everything

### **Proof Preferences**

1. **Local farmer testimonial** (most powerful)
2. Field trial results from his region
3. University studies (if from ag school)
4. Before/after photos from real farms
5. Money-back guarantees
6. Integrator approval/recommendation

### **Price vs. Quality Sensitivity**

- **Value-Conscious Calculator**:
- Will pay more IF clear ROI
- Thinks in "cents per bird" not total price
- Suspicious of both cheapest and most expensive
- Sweet spot: "Fair price for honest product"
- More willing to pay for prevention than treatment

### **Change Resistance Level**

- **High Initial Resistance, But Loyal Once Convinced**:
- Takes 3-5 touches before considering
- Needs multiple proof points
- Once converted, rarely switches
- Recommends to others after personal success

### **Success Celebration Style**

- Quietly proud, shares at coffee shop
- Posts pictures of good settlements on Facebook
- Mentions casually at church
- Never brags directly but lets numbers speak

### **Failure Coping**

- Blames external factors first (weather, feed quality, integrator)
- Seeks peer validation ("anyone else having issues?")
- Doubles down on what worked before
- Rarely admits mistake publicly

### **Key Identity Statement**

*"I'm a practical farmer who does what works. I'm not against new things, but I'm not a guinea pig either. Show me it works for guys like me, make it simple, and don't BS me with fancy talk."*
