# Lifestyle & Identity

## **Daily Routine/Schedule**

### **Grow-Out Days (Birds in House)**

- **4:30-5:00 AM**: Coffee, check overnight controller alerts on phone
- **5:00-7:00 AM**: First walk-through, mortality pickup, check feeders/drinkers
- **7:00-8:00 AM**: Breakfast with family, catch local farm report
- **8:00-11:00 AM**: House maintenance, repairs, adjustments based on bird behavior
- **11:00 AM-12:00 PM**: Lunch break, phone calls with other growers
- **12:00-2:00 PM**: Second walk-through, spot-check problem areas
- **2:00-5:00 PM**: Off-farm job (if applicable) OR equipment maintenance/prep
- **5:00-6:00 PM**: Evening walk-through, controller adjustments for night
- **6:00-7:00 PM**: Family dinner (sacred time)
- **7:00-8:00 PM**: Paperwork, Facebook, checking tomorrow's weather
- **8:00-10:00 PM**: Final house check, family time
- **10:00 PM**: Bed (will wake 1-2x to check controller alerts)

### **Between Flocks (Clean-Out/Setup)**

- **More flexible schedule**: 7 AM starts
- **Intensive work**: 10-12 hour days during clean-out
- **Planning period**: Equipment repairs, comparing notes with other growers
- **Family time**: Only window for vacations (rarely taken)

## **Work-Life Balance Priorities**

### **Non-Negotiables**

1. **Sunday church and family dinner** - Will only miss for emergencies
2. **Kids' major events** - Games, graduations (plans flocks around these)
3. **Daily family meals** - At least dinner together
4. **Hunting season** - 2-3 mornings between flocks

### **Sacrifices Accepted**

- Haven't taken real vacation in 5+ years
- Missed numerous social events due to sick birds
- Sleep interruption is normal (controller alerts)
- Holiday schedules dictated by bird placement

### **Integration Strategy**

- **Involves family**: Kids help with small tasks, learn responsibility
- **Wife manages books**: Often handles settlement paperwork
- **"Family business" mentality**: Everyone understands birds come first during crisis

## **Generational Farming Context**

### **Distribution Pattern** (Typical Mix)

- **40% Second Generation**: Dad had birds, expanded operation
- **35% First Generation Farmers**: Started from scratch 10-20 years ago
- **25% Career Switchers**: Former factory/military, bought into farming

### **Second Generation Characteristics**

- **Assets**: Inherited some houses, established integrator relationship
- **Pressure**: "Don't lose what Dad built"
- **Advantages**: Learned from childhood, established local credibility
- **Challenges**: Older facilities, family land disputes
- **Mindset**: "Granddad survived with 2 houses, I need 4-6 to make it"

### **First Generation Characteristics**

- **Entry**: Major debt to build houses ($1-2M investment)
- **Pressure**: "Make this work or lose everything"
- **Advantages**: Newer facilities, chose this life
- **Challenges**: Learning curve, no safety net
- **Mindset**: "I left steady paycheck for this, failure isn't option"

### **Career Switcher Characteristics**

- **Background**: Military retirement, factory closure, corporate burnout
- **Assets**: Outside income/pension helps with startup
- **Motivation**: "Want to work for myself, be outside"
- **Reality Check**: Shocked by 24/7 nature, integrator control
- **Adaptation**: Either fully commits or sells within 5 years

## **Life Stage Patterns**

### **Age 40-50 (Building Phase)**

- Peak physical capability for manual labor
- Kids at home help with operation
- Maximum debt tolerance
- Considering expansion or second income

### **Age 50-60 (Optimization Phase)**

- Perfecting system, less experimentation
- Thinking about succession planning
- Health issues starting (knees, back)
- Grandkids visiting the farm

### **Age 60-65+ (Transition Phase)**

- "How much longer can I do this?"
- Hoping kids take interest (usually don't)
- Exploring exit strategies
- More willing to try labor-saving solutions
