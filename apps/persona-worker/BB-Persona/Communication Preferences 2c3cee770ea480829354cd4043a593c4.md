# Communication Preferences

### **Channel Hierarchy & Usage Patterns**

### **Primary Channels (Daily Use)**

**Text Messaging (Most Responsive)**

- **Response Time**: 5-30 minutes during work hours
- **Best For**: Quick questions, updates, confirmations
- **Format**: Keep under 160 characters, no links
- **Don'ts**: Group texts, marketing language, after 9 PM
- **Sweet Spot**: "Bill, saw your birds yesterday. Looking good. Got a minute to talk about what you're doing different? -John"

**Phone Calls (Trust Builder)**

- **Best Times**: 6-7 AM (post-coffee), 12-1 PM (lunch), 7-8 PM (settled)
- **Worst Times**: 5-6 AM (walking houses), 4-6 PM (evening chores)
- **Call Length**: 5-10 minutes unless he's engaged
- **Approach**: Start with farming talk, not product
- **Voicemail**: Under 30 seconds, specific callback time

**Face-to-Face (Decision Maker)**

- **Best Locations**: His farm, feed store, coffee shop
- **Worst Locations**: Formal office, restaurant
- **Timing**: Between flocks, Saturday mornings
- **Protocol**: Always call before dropping by
- **Power Move**: Bring donuts at 6 AM

### **Secondary Channels (Regular Use)**

**Facebook (Passive Consumer)**

- **Active Times**: 7-9 PM, Sunday afternoons
- **Behavior**: Lurks 95%, posts 5%
- **Engagement**: Likes photos, rarely comments
- **Groups**: 2-3 poultry groups, local community
- **Content**: Success photos, not problems

**WhatsApp/GroupMe (Grower Groups)**

- **Usage**: Complex-specific groups
- **Activity**: Reads everything, posts little
- **Topics**: Weather alerts, disease warnings
- **Value**: Real-time local intel

### **Tertiary Channels (Occasional)**

**Email (Document Only)**

- **Check Frequency**: 2-3x per week
- **Purpose**: Settlement statements, official docs
- **Response Rate**: <20% unless urgent
- **Format**: Subject line must be clear/urgent

**YouTube (Problem-Solving)**

- **Usage**: Specific equipment repair
- **Preference**: Under 5 minutes, practical
- **Trust**: Farmers/techs, not companies

### **Information Consumption Habits**

### **Daily Information Diet**

**5:00-6:30 AM (Peak Receptivity)**

- **Medium**: Local radio farm report
- **Topics**: Weather, grain prices, ag news
- **Mindset**: Planning day, open to ideas
- **Opportunity**: Best time for new information

**12:00-1:00 PM (Social Hour)**

- **Medium**: Phone calls with growers
- **Topics**: Comparing notes, troubleshooting
- **Mindset**: Problem-solving mode
- **Opportunity**: Peer validation time

**7:00-9:00 PM (Wind Down)**

- **Medium**: Facebook, maybe YouTube
- **Topics**: Success stories, community news
- **Mindset**: Relaxed, reflective
- **Opportunity**: Educational content

**Throughout Day (Passive)**

- **Radio** in truck: Country station with farm reports
- **Podcasts**: None - "That's city people stuff"
- **Print**: Poultry Times in bathroom

### **Seasonal Information Patterns**

**Winter (December-February)**

- **Mode**: Research and planning
- **Receptivity**: Highest for new products
- **Content**: Attends meetings, reads more
- **Decisions**: Next year's protocols

**Spring (March-May)**

- **Mode**: Implementation
- **Receptivity**: Low for new, high for reminders
- **Content**: Quick tips, refreshers
- **Decisions**: Minor adjustments only

**Summer (June-August)**

- **Mode**: Crisis management
- **Receptivity**: Emergency solutions only
- **Content**: Heat stress, disease prevention
- **Decisions**: Reactive, immediate

**Fall (September-November)**

- **Mode**: Evaluation
- **Receptivity**: Moderate for improvements
- **Content**: Year reviews, planning
- **Decisions**: What worked, what didn't

### **Trusted Authority Hierarchy**

### **Tier 1: Gospel Truth**

**Top Local Grower**

- *"If Randy's doing it, it works"*
- Must be within 50 miles
- Similar operation size
- 2+ years proven success
- Personal relationship

**Experienced Integrator Field Tech**

- *"John's seen a thousand flocks"*
- 10+ years with company
- Grew up local
- Doesn't push products
- Shares inside information

### **Tier 2: Highly Trusted**

**Successful Neighbor Farmers**

- 3-4 guys in coffee group
- Compare settlements regularly
- Share successes and failures
- No competitive secret keeping

**Long-Term Feed Store Owner**

- *"Bob's been selling to farmers for 30 years"*
- Knows what works locally
- Stands behind recommendations
- Fair pricing, honest advice

**Local Veterinarian**

- State-licensed poultry specialist
- Works with integrator
- Practical, not academic
- Available for emergencies

### **Tier 3: Cautiously Trusted**

**Extension Service**

- If from state ag school
- If has poultry experience
- If recommendations are practical
- Not if "too academic"

**Equipment Dealer Tech**

- Knows specific equipment
- Fixed problems before
- Doesn't oversell
- Local, not corporate

### **Tier 4: Skeptically Evaluated**

**Sales Representatives**

- Need 3+ consistent interactions
- Must know poultry, not just product
- Local/regional, not national
- Relationship before transaction

**University Research**

- In-state schools preferred
- Must include local conditions
- Farmer-partnered studies best
- Not lab-only results

### **Tier 5: Generally Distrusted**

**Online Experts**

- "YouTube farmers"
- Consultants without local experience
- Corporate representatives
- Anyone too polished/smooth

### **Skepticism Triggers (Instant Distrust)**

### **Language Red Flags**

**Corporate Speak**

- "Synergistic solutions"
- "Paradigm shift"
- "Disruptive technology"
- "Best practices"
- "Leverage your assets"

**Overpromising**

- "Guaranteed to..."
- "Eliminate all problems"
- "Revolutionary breakthrough"
- "Never seen before results"
- "Triple your profits"

**Environmental Virtue Signaling**

- "Save the planet"
- "Sustainable agriculture"
- "Carbon neutral"
- "Eco-warrior"
- "Green revolution"

### **Messenger Red Flags**

**Personal Presentation**

- Suit and tie on farm
- Clean boots (never worked)
- Rental car with out-of-state plates
- Too young ("What's this kid know?")
- Too educated ("Book smart, not farm smart")

**Behavioral Triggers**

- Doesn't ask about his operation first
- Can't answer basic poultry questions
- Pushy or time-pressured
- Name drops strangers
- Criticizes current methods

### **Product/Company Red Flags**

- West Coast company
- No local dealers
- Requires technology/apps
- Multi-level marketing structure
- New company (<5 years)
- Foreign-made
- No money-back guarantee

### **Preferred Proof Types (Hierarchy of Evidence)**

### **Gold Standard Proof**

**Neighbor's Settlement Check**

- *"Jim showed me his settlement - $3,000 better"*
- Actual document, not claim
- Similar house/bird count
- Same integrator
- Recent (last 2 flocks)

**Side-by-Side House Comparison**

- Test house vs. control house
- Same farm, same management
- Clear photos/documentation
- Multiple flock consistency

### **Silver Standard Proof**

**Local Field Trial**

- Within 100 miles
- Similar climate/conditions
- Named farmers (can call)
- University or integrator supervised
- Full flock cycle data

**Before/After Photos**

- Same farm, dated
- Clear visible improvement
- Farmer-taken (not professional)
- Multiple angles/examples

### **Bronze Standard Proof**

**Testimonials With Details**

- Full name and location
- Specific numbers/results
- Contact information provided
- Video better than written
- Recent (<1 year old)

**University Research**

- State ag school preferred
- Poultry-specific department
- Field trials, not lab only
- Published in trade journal

### **Acceptable Supporting Proof**

**ROI Calculations**

- Simple cost per bird
- Based on local prices
- Conservative estimates
- Clear break-even point

**Company History**

- Years serving poultry
- Number of local customers
- Company stability/growth
- Owner background

### **Message Format Preferences**

### **Optimal Message Structure**

**Opening (10 seconds)**

- Local/peer reference
- Specific problem acknowledgment
- Credibility statement

**Middle (30 seconds)**

- Single main benefit
- Local proof point
- Simple numbers

**Close (10 seconds)**

- Soft call to action
- No pressure
- Leave something tangible

### **Content Format Preferences**

**Best Formats**:

- One-page sell sheets with photos
- Comparison charts (2-3 items max)
- Cost-per-bird calculations
- Local success stories (1-page)

**Acceptable Formats**:

- Short videos (<3 minutes)
- Brochures with testimonials
- Sample products to try
- Research summaries (1-page)

**Rejected Formats**:

- PowerPoint presentations
- White papers
- Email newsletters
- Webinars
- Podcasts

### **Response Patterns & Timing**

### **Decision Communication Timeline**

**First Contact Response**

- Polite but non-committal
- "I'll think about it"
- Won't say no directly
- Asks about neighbors using it

**Follow-Up Response** (2-3 weeks)

- More specific questions
- Price/ROI focused
- Mentions to coffee group
- May ask for test amount

**Decision Communication**

- Calls you (not vice versa)
- Wants to "try a little"
- Asks about delivery
- Brings up payment terms

### **Communication Don'ts**

**Never**:

- Call during church (Sunday AM)
- Text after 9 PM
- Email anything urgent
- Use group messaging
- Share his information
- Mention competitors negatively
- Pressure for immediate decision
- Skip the relationship building
- Talk down to him
- Use environmental guilt

### **Effective Message Templates**

### **Initial Text**

*"Bill, this is Mike from [Company]. Jim Taylor suggested I reach out. He's seen good results with mortality this season. Could I give you a quick call tomorrow morning around 6:30? No pressure, just sharing what's working for others around here."*

### **Voicemail**

*"Bill, Mike from [Company]. Jim Taylor gave me your number - said you run a tight operation over there. Got something that helped him drop mortality by 1% last flock. I'll try you again tomorrow afternoon, or shoot me a text when's good for you."*

### **Facebook Message**

*"Hey Bill, saw your comment in the Georgia Poultry Group about mortality challenges. Several guys around [County] have had success with [specific solution]. Happy to share what they're doing if you're interested. No sales pitch, just grower to grower information."*

### **Conversation Flow Patterns**

### **Successful Conversation Arc**

1. **Personal connection** (weather, family, farming)
2. **His operation** (ask about his birds/challenges)
3. **Peer success** (what neighbors are doing)
4. **Soft introduction** (product mention)
5. **Evidence sharing** (proof points)
6. **Trial offer** (small test)
7. **No pressure close** (call me when ready)

### **Trust-Building Timeline**

- **Contact 1**: Introduction, no selling
- **Contact 2**: Share information, build rapport
- **Contact 3**: Offer trial, provide proof
- **Contact 4**: Follow up on trial
- **Contact 5**: Expand if successful
