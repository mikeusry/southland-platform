## Ammonia: The Number You Can't Ignore

Ammonia is the single biggest air quality challenge in commercial poultry production. Above 25 ppm, it damages the respiratory lining, opening the door to secondary infections. Above 50 ppm, it measurably reduces growth rate and feed conversion. Most growers can't detect ammonia by smell until it's already above 20 ppm — by then, birds have been breathing it for days.

Litter Life attacks the problem at the source. It shifts the microbial balance in the litter so less ammonia is generated in the first place — rather than masking it after the fact.

## The Economics of Better Litter

Every cleanout costs $3,000-5,000 when you factor in labor, equipment, bedding, and downtime. If Litter Life extends your litter by 2-3 flocks, that's one fewer cleanout per year — $3,000-5,000 back in your pocket.

Add the paw quality improvement (fewer plant downgrades, access to export premiums) and the livability gains from better air quality, and the ROI compounds quickly. Most growers report that Litter Life pays for itself within the first flock.
