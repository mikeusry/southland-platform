## The Problem Under Your Lawn

Most lawn problems aren't grass problems — they're soil problems. Southern soils, especially heavy clay, compact over time. Microbial populations decline. Nutrients lock up in forms that roots can't access. You apply more fertilizer, but the soil can't deliver it.

Humates break this cycle by restoring the biological engine in your soil. Humic acid chelates locked-up minerals, feeds the beneficial bacteria and fungi that naturally aerate soil, and improves the cation exchange capacity (CEC) so nutrients stay in the root zone instead of leaching away.

## What Humic Acid Does That Fertilizer Can't

Fertilizer puts nutrients on the field. Humates help the soil hold and deliver those nutrients to roots. Research from the Soil Science Society shows that humic substances can improve nutrient uptake efficiency by 20-40%. That means you get more from every dollar you spend on fertility.

For turf professionals, this translates directly to the bottom line: better results with lower input costs. For homeowners, it means a lawn that actually responds to what you put on it.

## Works With What You're Already Doing

Humates aren't a replacement for your fertilizer program — they make it work better. They're tank-mix compatible with most liquid fertilizers, herbicides, and fungicides. Apply them on your existing schedule with any hose-end, pump, or boom sprayer. No special equipment needed.
