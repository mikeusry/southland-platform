## Why We Built a Natural Insecticide

The conventional approach to pest control is simple: kill everything with chemicals and deal with the consequences later. But those consequences add up — toxic residue on countertops, dead pollinators in the garden, kids and pets banished from treated areas for hours after every application.

We built Defender for the people we hear from most: small-farm owners and homeowners who want bugs gone but won't spray something they can't use around their family. Its natural active ingredients work on contact — a broad-spectrum knockdown that hits ants, roaches, spiders, flies, mosquitoes, and common garden pests — then break down without leaving harmful residue behind. That's the whole idea: pest control that works in the spaces where your family actually lives.

## How It Works — Contact Kill, No Lingering Residue

Defender is a contact insecticide. It affects the insects it directly touches rather than coating your home in a persistent chemical film, which is exactly why there's no evacuation period and no re-entry interval. You spray, the active ingredients do their job, and what's left breaks down instead of sitting on your countertops or leaching into your soil and water.

That mechanism is also its one honest trade-off: because it works on contact and doesn't linger, you reapply more often than you would with a synthetic residual — every 2–4 weeks indoors, or after heavy rain outdoors. For most of our customers, reapplying a product they trust beats living with one they don't.

Because it isn't selective, we tell people to be deliberate about pollinators: spray in early morning or evening, and avoid hitting bees and ladybugs directly. The same contact action that drops a roach will affect a beneficial insect you spray it on.

## One Product, Every Space on the Property

Most households end up with three products — one for indoor bugs, one for the garden, one for the barn — each with its own safety rules. Defender handles all three. Spray baseboards and entry points for ants, hit garden beds and foliage for aphids, spray animal housing and fence lines for flies — same bottle, same safety profile, no switching between chemical formulations or remembering which one is safe where.

For farms and homesteads where animals, children, and gardens share the same ground, that simplicity is the point. It's plant-safe enough for ornamentals, vegetables, and fruit trees without phytotoxicity concerns, and safe enough around poultry and livestock that you don't have to clear the barn to use it. (For a poultry-house pest program built specifically around darkling beetles, we point growers to [Desecticide](/products/desecticide) — but for everyday pest control across the whole property, Defender is the one bottle we reach for.)
