## The Insecticide Our Own Spray Crews Trust

Most "natural" pest products are something a brand bought, bottled, and hoped would work. Defender is different: it's the formula our own mosquito and tick service, [Spray Squad](https://www.spraysquad.com/), has been spraying on real properties since 2017 — now across 500+ Georgia families and eleven counties. We don't recommend it from a lab. We recommend it because we've carried it on the truck for years and put our own service name behind every application.

That field history is the whole point. When a crew sprays the same yards every season, a product that doesn't perform gets dropped fast. Defender stayed.

## What's Actually In It — and Why It Works

Defender's active ingredients are essential oils: **citronella, peppermint, and cinnamon oil**. That isn't a marketing softener — it's the working chemistry. These oils are a contact insecticide: they hit an insect's nervous system on contact and drop it, then break down instead of leaving a synthetic residue on your counters, in your soil, or in your water.

Because the actives are recognized minimum-risk ingredients, Defender qualifies as an **EPA 25(b) minimum-risk pesticide** — the federal category for products considered low enough risk that they're exempt from full EPA registration. In plain terms: it's built from ingredients the EPA already regards as safe enough to skip the registration gauntlet, which is exactly why there's no evacuation period and no re-entry interval.

This same essential-oil formula class has been put through the wringer: in independent testing it has shown an **80%+ kill rate on target pests and larvae within 24 hours**, knocking down mosquitoes on contact and controlling ticks — performance measured against World Health Organization testing standards, and in head-to-head trials it has gone toe-to-toe with leading synthetic sprays. You get that knockdown without the toxic trade-offs synthetics carry.

## The Honest Trade-Off

Because Defender works on contact and deliberately doesn't linger, you reapply more often than you would with a synthetic residual — every 2–4 weeks indoors, or after heavy rain outdoors. We'd rather tell you that up front than pretend a safe-around-your-kids product behaves like a chemical film that lingers for months. For most of our customers — and for our own crews — reapplying something they trust beats living with something they don't.

One more honest note: contact action isn't selective. The same hit that drops a roach will affect a pollinator you spray directly, so spray in early morning or evening and avoid hitting bees and ladybugs head-on.

## One Bottle, Every Space on the Property

Most households end up juggling three products — one for indoor bugs, one for the garden, one for the barn — each with its own safety rules. Defender handles all three. Spray baseboards and entry points for ants, hit garden beds and foliage for aphids, spray animal housing and fence lines for flies — same bottle, same safety profile, indoors and out.

For farms and homesteads where animals, children, and gardens share the same ground, that simplicity is the point. It's plant-safe enough for ornamentals, vegetables, and fruit trees without phytotoxicity concerns, and gentle enough around poultry and livestock that you don't have to clear the barn to use it. (For a poultry-house program built specifically around darkling beetles, we point growers to [Desecticide](/products/desecticide) — but for everyday pest control across the whole property, Defender is the one bottle our crews reach for.)
