## How to Fix Dog Urine Spots on Your Lawn

Dog urine burns grass because it delivers far more nitrogen than the grass can use. A little nitrogen is fertilizer, but a concentrated spot of dog pee overwhelms the grass and burns it — leaving the brown patch, often ringed by darker green grass where the urine was diluted enough to fertilize instead. Fixing it takes two things: **deal with the excess nitrogen, and rebuild the soil so grass can regrow.**

Dog Spot does both. Built on humic acid, active carbon, and beneficial microbes, it encapsulates the excess nitrogen and repairs the soil underneath. Dilute 4 oz per gallon of water and apply to the brown spots. Existing grass at the edges fills back in, and dead areas are prepped for reseeding.

## All-Natural — Safe for Pets and Kids

Dog Spot repairs your lawn with humic acid, active carbon, and beneficial microbes — not harsh chemicals — so it's safe for the dogs and kids who use the yard.

## For Large Lawns: Use Genesis

If you have a big lawn with many dog urine spots, **Genesis** is the more cost-effective choice — the same soil-repair approach in a gallon size. Treat the whole problem area rather than chasing individual spots.
