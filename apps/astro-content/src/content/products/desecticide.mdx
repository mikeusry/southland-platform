## The Hidden Cost of Darkling Beetles

Darkling beetles aren't just a nuisance — they're a profit leak. In commercial poultry houses, beetle infestations create three compounding problems:

**Disease transmission.** Beetles are confirmed carriers of E. coli, Salmonella, Marek's disease, avian influenza, and other pathogens. They reinfect each new flock from the litter, creating a persistent disease cycle that probiotics and vaccines alone can't fully address.

**Feed loss.** Industry estimates suggest heavily infested houses may lose 5-10% of feed to beetle consumption. For a 20,000-bird broiler house, that can mean an estimated $400-800 per flock going to insects.

**Structural damage.** Beetle larvae bore into insulation, wooden posts, and styrofoam panels. Over time, this compromises your house's thermal envelope, increasing energy costs and requiring expensive repairs.

## Why Natural Matters

Traditional chemical insecticides leave residue in the house environment, can affect bird health, and often require withdrawal periods or empty-house application windows. Desecticide uses natural active ingredients that target beetle biology without chemical persistence.

This means you can treat during production — not just at cleanout — giving you a continuous control tool instead of periodic knockdown.

## Part of a Biosecurity Program

Desecticide works best as part of an integrated approach. Many growers combine it with Big Ole Bird (probiotics for gut health), Litter Life (litter amendment for ammonia and pathogen control), and Catalyst (vitamin supplement) for comprehensive flock health management.
