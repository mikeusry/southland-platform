## Built by athletes who lived in this gear

JockShock was built by people who actually used it first — D1 collegiate athletes who lived through the gear bags, the locker rooms, the smell that stays in the car after practice. The product exists because the founders couldn't find a deodorizer that was strong enough to kill the bacteria, safe enough to use on every piece of gear, and built for an after-every-practice routine instead of occasional damage control.

## The chemistry: hypochlorous, not fragrance

Most "gear sprays" mask odor with fragrance. JockShock kills the bacteria that produce odor at the source. The active ingredient is hypochlorous acid (HOCl) — an EPA-registered antimicrobial produced under ZeroPoint's subregistration. HOCl is the same molecule the human immune system produces to kill pathogens. It's strong enough to be clinical-grade, gentle enough to be safe on the materials in athletic gear.

## Routine, not rescue

The honest truth: a single bottle of any gear spray won't fix a locker room that's been ignored for a season. JockShock is built around a different model — spray after every practice, every game, every gear use. The 32 oz bottle is sized for a real daily routine. It dries clean, doesn't bleach jerseys, and fits in a gear bag.
