## Why Peracetic Acid Is Replacing Chlorine in Food Processing

For decades, chlorine-based sanitizers were the standard in food processing and production. But peracetic acid is rapidly becoming the preferred antimicrobial for facilities that need reliable kill performance without the drawbacks: no toxic residues, no temperature sensitivity, and no biofilm blind spots.

PAA works by oxidizing cell membranes on contact. Unlike chlorine, it doesn't lose effectiveness in cold environments or in the presence of organic matter. And unlike quaternary ammonium compounds, it leaves no persistent residue — it breaks down to acetic acid (vinegar) and water.

## The Biofilm Problem Most Sanitizers Miss

Standard surface sanitizers kill exposed bacteria on clean surfaces. But in real production environments, pathogens like Listeria monocytogenes and Salmonella form biofilms — protective layers that shield colonies from chemical exposure. These biofilms survive routine cleaning and re-seed contamination cycle after cycle.

Peracetic acid penetrates biofilm matrices and kills the organisms inside. This makes PAA uniquely effective for CIP systems, drain sanitation, and any surface where biofilm formation is a recurring risk.

## Cold Chain Confidence

Refrigerated environments are where contamination risk meets sanitizer weakness. Most chemical sanitizers lose potency below 40F. Peracetic acid doesn't. Whether you're sanitizing cold storage floors, refrigerated processing equipment, or produce wash systems, Alpet PAA 5.6% delivers consistent kill performance at the temperatures where it matters most.
