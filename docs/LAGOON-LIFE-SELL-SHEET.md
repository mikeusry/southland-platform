**Biological Lagoon Treatment for Commercial Swine Operations**

## The Problem: Conventional Treatments Don't Reach the Waste

Your lagoon has accumulated years of compacted sludge under an impenetrable biofilm crust. Every bacterial treatment you've tried dumps biology on top of that crust — the bacteria can't reach the waste they're supposed to digest. They float on the surface, die, and odor returns in weeks.

**The result:** Hydrogen sulfide complaints, regulatory violations, nutrient loading issues, and costly mechanical pump-outs ($50K--$150K per event).

---

## The Lagoon Life Difference: Three-Part Biological Action

### 1. Surfactin Penetration (500 ppm)

- **Biological surfactant** from *Bacillus subtilis* fermentation — not chemical detergents
- **500 ppm concentration** — 10x higher than typical biosurfactant applications
- **Breaks biofilm matrix** and emulsifies fats, greases, and hydrophobic organics
- **Reduces surface tension** from 72 mN/m to 27 mN/m
- **Opens pathways** into compacted sludge layer for bacterial colonization

### 2. Fulvic Acid Carrier (Histosol)

- **Delivers micronutrients** to feed introduced biology
- **Buffers pH** in acidic lagoon conditions (maintains 6.5--8.5 range)
- **Chelates heavy metals** away from bacterial activity zones
- **Increases cation exchange capacity** for enhanced nutrient processing

### 3. Anaerobic/Facultative Microbial Consortium

Lagoon-adapted *Bacillus* species that thrive in oxygen-depleted, high-organic-load environments:

- **Digests organic solids** — reduces sludge volume and extends lagoon capacity
- **Oxidizes hydrogen sulfide** — converts H2S to harmless sulfate, eliminates odor at source
- **Processes nitrogen** — nitrification and denitrification reduce ammonia emissions
- **Solubilizes phosphorus** — supports nutrient compliance requirements
- **Competitively excludes pathogens** — reduces Salmonella, E. coli, Clostridium perfringens
- **Produces surfactin in situ** — self-sustaining biology maintains its own penetration agent

## Provisional Application Protocol

*Subject to refinement during pilot testing*

### Shock Treatment (Initial Application)

- **Purpose:** Break biofilm crust, penetrate sludge layer, establish microbial colony
- **Dosing:** 1 gallon Lagoon Life per 250,000 gallons lagoon volume
- **Application:** Edge-pour from lagoon perimeter or spray from bank using standard equipment
- **Frequency:** Single application; reassess after 30 days

**Example:** 2-million-gallon lagoon = 8 gallons (approximately 4x 2.5G jugs) at shock dose

### Maintenance Treatment (Monthly)

- **Purpose:** Sustain microbial population, prevent biofilm re-formation, ongoing sludge digestion
- **Dosing:** 1 gallon per 500,000--1,000,000 gallons lagoon volume monthly
- **Application:** Same edge-pour or spray method
- **Frequency:** Monthly during biological activity season (>50 F)

**Example:** 2-million-gallon lagoon = 2--4 gallons monthly at maintenance dose

### Seasonal Considerations

- **Below 50 F:** Biological activity slows significantly; pause treatment until spring or increase dose
- **Peak summer (80 F+):** Highest biological activity; standard or reduced maintenance dose
- **Fall/Spring:** Optimal treatment windows; biology active, lagoon not thermally stratified

## Expected Outcomes (Based on 90-Day Pilot Protocol)

### Primary Benefits

- **>=50% reduction in hydrogen sulfide (H2S)** at lagoon surface and downwind sampling points
- **20--30% reduction in average sludge depth** without mechanical agitation
- **Visible disruption of surface crust** within 7--14 days of shock treatment
- **Measurable decreases in TSS, BOD5, total nitrogen, and total phosphorus**

### Operational Benefits

- **Extends lagoon capacity** — defers or eliminates costly mechanical pump-outs
- **Reduces odor complaints** — addresses H2S at the source through competitive exclusion
- **Supports regulatory compliance** — reduces nutrient loading in lagoon effluent
- **Self-sustaining biology** — ongoing surfactin production maintains treatment effectiveness

### Economic Benefits

Competing products rely on continual re-dosing of chemical surfactants. Lagoon Life's *Bacillus* strains produce surfactin in situ after colonization, so penetration power is biologically self-maintaining while your ongoing chemical input stays flat or decreases. The initial shock dose breaks the crust; the biology sustains itself from there.
