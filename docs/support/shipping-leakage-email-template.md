# Support Email Template — "My order arrived leaking"

Use this canned reply when a customer reports that a liquid product arrived
leaking or seeping. It points to the support page (which hosts the explainer
video, the full explanation, and an FAQ) instead of pasting the raw Mux link.

**Support page:** https://southlandorganics.com/support/shipping-leakage/
**Use this URL — not** `https://player.mux.com/LXPYGvvlC9vECKIhHwwdvnHNrJ01rk302gf5IcAe8EG02Y`
(the bare Mux link gives the customer a single video with no context; the page
explains the policy, embeds the same video, and tells them what to do next).

---

## Template (copy/paste)

> Subject: Re: Order arrived damaged - leaking
>
> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> Thank you for reaching out, and for sending photos — we're glad to see the
> product arrived alive and well!
>
> A small amount of leakage can happen with our products in transit. They're
> living, biological liquids, and changes in temperature and pressure during
> shipping can push a little product past the cap seal. Because we know this
> can happen, **we fill every bottle with extra volume to compensate** — so even
> with some seepage, you should still have the full amount of product you paid
> for.
>
> We put together a short page that explains why this happens, includes a quick
> video from our team, and answers the most common questions:
>
> 👉 https://southlandorganics.com/support/shipping-leakage/
>
> Just wipe the bottle down, give it a gentle shake, and use it as directed. If
> you measure it out and feel you're genuinely short, let us know your order
> number and we'll make it right.
>
> Please don't hesitate to reach out with any questions!
>
> Thank you!
>
> {{agent_signature}}

---

## Variables to fill in
- `{{first_name}}` — customer's first name
- `{{agent_signature}}` — the agent's standard email signature block

## Notes
- Keep the **support page URL**, drop the raw Mux player URL.
- The video on the page is the same one previously linked directly — customers
  who only want the video still get it, with context around it.
- If the customer later confirms they're short, escalate per normal
  replacement/refund process (order number required).
