# 90-Day Pilot Study Protocol

**Lagoon Life Bioaugmentation Efficacy in Commercial Swine Lagoons**

---

## Study Objective

Demonstrate that Lagoon Life biological lagoon treatment:

1. Reduces hydrogen sulfide (H2S) odor by >=50% at lagoon surface and downwind monitoring points
2. Reduces average sludge depth by >=20--30% without mechanical agitation
3. Decreases total suspended solids (TSS), biochemical oxygen demand (BOD5), total nitrogen (TKN), and total phosphorus (TP) relative to baseline

---

## Site Selection Criteria

### Ideal Pilot Lagoon

- **Size:** 1--5 million gallon capacity (medium commercial operation)
- **Location:** North Carolina, South Carolina, or Georgia (Smithfield-affiliated or similar integrator)
- **Documented Issues:**
  - Active odor complaints (H2S detectable at property line)
  - Sludge accumulation >2 feet average depth
  - Consideration of mechanical pump-out within next 12--24 months
- **Operational Status:** Active lagoon receiving continuous waste input from finishing or farrow-to-finish barn
- **Access:** Safe perimeter access for sampling; boat or waders available for sludge depth measurements
- **Baseline Data Availability:** Historical lagoon maintenance records (last pump-out date, previous sludge surveys) preferred but not required

### Exclusion Criteria

- Lagoons receiving industrial co-waste (rendering, processing plant discharge) that may interfere with microbial activity
- Lagoons treated with chemical biocides, algaecides, or competing bioaugmentation products within previous 60 days
- Lagoons scheduled for closure or major construction during study period

---

## Study Design

### Timeline

- **Day 0:** Baseline sampling and measurements
- **Day 1:** Shock treatment application (Lagoon Life)
- **Day 30:** First follow-up sampling
- **Day 60:** Second follow-up sampling
- **Day 90:** Final sampling and pilot completion

### Treatment Protocol

**Shock Dose (Day 1):**

- Apply Lagoon Life at **1 gallon per 250,000 gallons lagoon volume**
- Application method: Edge-pour at 8--12 evenly spaced locations around lagoon perimeter, or spray from bank using pump sprayer

**Maintenance Dose (Days 30 and 60):**

- Apply Lagoon Life at **1 gallon per 500,000--1,000,000 gallons lagoon volume**
- Same application method as shock dose

**Weather Monitoring:**

- Record daily high/low temperatures and precipitation during study period
- Note any extreme events (lagoon turnover, heavy rainfall, freeze events)

## Sampling and Measurement Protocol

### A. Hydrogen Sulfide (H2S) and Ammonia (NH3) Gas Monitoring

**Equipment:**

- Portable gas detector (e.g., Industrial Scientific GasBadge Pro, Jerome 631-X H2S analyzer) or Drager-Tube colorimetric system
- Calibration gases and field blanks

**Sampling Points:**

- **Lagoon surface (5 fixed points):** Establish a uniform grid covering lagoon surface area; measure H2S concentration at ~6 inches above liquid surface from boat or lagoon edge
- **Downwind perimeter (2 points):** Position at property line or 100 feet downwind from lagoon edge (prevailing wind direction); measure at 4--6 feet above ground level (breathing zone)

**Timing:**

- All measurements taken between 6:00--10:00 AM (peak odor emission period)
- Conducted on days 0, 30, 60, 90

**Data Recording:**

- H2S concentration (ppm) at each point
- NH3 concentration (ppm) if equipment available
- Wind speed and direction, ambient temperature, time of day

**Target Outcome:** >=50% reduction in mean H2S concentration at lagoon surface and downwind points by Day 90 relative to Day 0

### B. Sludge Depth Measurement

**Equipment:**

- Sludge Judge or clear PVC pipe (2-inch diameter) with bottom flap valve (Nasco Sampling Products or equivalent)
- Marked measuring tape
- GPS or marked grid stakes for repeatable sampling locations

**Sampling Grid:**

- Minimum **6 measurement points per acre** of lagoon surface area (e.g., 2-acre lagoon = 12 points minimum)
- Establish uniform rectangular grid pattern; mark coordinates on lagoon map
- Use same exact grid locations for all sampling events (GPS coordinates or permanent shore markers)

**Measurement Procedure:**

1. Lower Sludge Judge slowly through liquid layer until liquid level inside pipe drops below outside level (indicates sludge layer contact)
2. Record depth from liquid surface to sludge interface (liquid depth)
3. If possible, push pipe to lagoon bottom and record total depth; sludge depth = total depth - liquid depth
4. Retain ~2 inches of sludge in bottom of pipe to confirm sludge layer reached

**Data Recording:**

- Liquid depth (feet) at each grid point
- Sludge depth (feet) at each grid point
- Calculate mean sludge depth across all points
- Map sludge distribution (optional: use GIS or simple spreadsheet heatmap)

**Timing:**

- Days 0, 30, 60, 90

**Target Outcome:** >=20--30% reduction in mean sludge depth by Day 90 without mechanical agitation

### C. Water Quality Sampling and Laboratory Analysis

**Sampling Locations:**

- **Lagoon composite sample:** Collect 1-liter grab samples from 3--5 locations across lagoon surface (avoiding immediate edges); combine into single composite sample
- **Mid-depth sample (optional):** If feasible, collect sample from mid-depth zone (~3 feet below surface) to assess stratification

**Parameters for Laboratory Analysis:**

| Parameter | Method | Target Detection | Regulatory Context |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Total Suspended Solids (TSS) | EPA Method 160.2 | mg/L | Lagoon discharge limits, sludge accumulation indicator |
| Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD5) | EPA Method 405.1 (5-day) | mg/L | Organic load, treatment efficiency |
| Total Kjeldahl Nitrogen (TKN) | EPA Method 351.2 | mg/L | Nitrogen loading, ammonia precursor |
| Ammonia-Nitrogen (NH3-N) | EPA Method 350.1 | mg/L | Volatilization risk, odor contributor |
| Nitrate-Nitrogen (NO3-N) | EPA Method 353.2 | mg/L | Nitrification indicator |
| Total Phosphorus (TP) | EPA Method 365.1 | mg/L | Nutrient runoff compliance |
| pH | Field meter (EPA Method 150.1) | 6.5--8.5 range | Biological activity window |

**Sample Handling:**

- Collect in clean HDPE bottles provided by laboratory
- Store on ice in cooler; deliver to lab within 24 hours or according to lab chain-of-custody requirements
- Follow laboratory-specific preservation protocols for each parameter

**Laboratory:**

- Use certified commercial environmental laboratory (e.g., Midwest Laboratories, Waters Agricultural Laboratories, or state-certified local lab)
- Request standard lagoon nutrient analysis package + TSS/BOD

**Timing:**

- Days 0, 30, 60, 90

**Target Outcome:** Measurable decreases in TSS, BOD5, TKN, and TP by Day 90 relative to baseline

### D. Visual and Operational Observations

**Recorded Weekly (or as observed):**

- Surface crust thickness and coverage area (estimate % of surface covered)
- Visible color changes in lagoon liquid (brown/black sludge vs. lighter treated zones)
- Odor intensity (subjective scale: none, slight, moderate, strong, very strong)
- Fly and insect activity around lagoon perimeter
- Any foam, scum, or floating solids accumulation
- Pump operation issues (clogging, reduced suction, flow rate changes)
- Neighbor or worker complaints logged during study period

**Photographic Documentation:**

- Take photos from same vantage points on Days 0, 7, 14, 30, 60, 90
- Include date/time stamps

## Data Analysis and Success Criteria

### Primary Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **H2S Reduction** | >=50% decrease in mean H2S concentration (ppm) at lagoon surface and downwind points | Day 90 vs. Day 0 |
| **Sludge Depth Reduction** | >=20% decrease in mean sludge depth (feet) across grid points | Day 90 vs. Day 0 |
| **TSS Reduction** | >=15% decrease in TSS (mg/L) | Day 90 vs. Day 0 |

### Secondary Success Criteria

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| **BOD5 Reduction** | >=10% decrease |
| **Total Nitrogen (TKN) Reduction** | >=10% decrease |
| **Total Phosphorus Reduction** | >=5% decrease |
| **Surface Crust Disruption** | Visible crust breakup within 14 days of shock treatment |
| **Odor Complaints** | Reduction in logged complaints during study period vs. previous 90 days |

### Statistical Analysis

- Calculate percent change from baseline for all quantitative metrics
- Report mean +/- standard deviation for multi-point measurements (H2S, sludge depth)
- Use paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test to assess significance of Day 0 vs. Day 90 changes (p < 0.05)

## Deliverables

### Pilot Study Report (Provided by Southland Organics)

1. **Executive Summary:** One-page overview of lagoon characteristics, treatment protocol, and key findings
2. **Data Tables and Figures:**
   - H2S and NH3 concentration trends (line graph, Day 0--90)
   - Sludge depth heatmap or contour plot showing spatial distribution
   - Water quality parameter trends (TSS, BOD, N, P)
3. **Statistical Analysis:** Percent reductions and significance testing
4. **Photographic Evidence:** Before/after lagoon appearance
5. **Operational Observations:** Crust disruption timeline, odor changes, pump performance
6. **Conclusions and Recommendations:** Dosing optimization for full-scale deployment, cost-benefit analysis, next steps

### Operator Feedback Form (Provided by Cooperating Farm)

- Weekly checklist: odor intensity, visual changes, complaints, pump operation
- Optional: lagoon maintenance cost savings estimate (deferred pump-out value)

### Cooperating Farm/Operator

- Assist with lagoon measurements as needed (boat access, safety support)
- Record weekly operational observations using provided checklist
- Report any unusual events (weather, lagoon turnover, equipment failures) during study period

---

## Safety and Regulatory Compliance

- All sampling activities conducted in compliance with OSHA confined space and hazardous gas exposure regulations (1910.146)
- Personnel equipped with H2S personal monitors when working near lagoon edge or in boat
- No entry into lagoon liquid; all sampling from perimeter or boat
- Pilot conducted under existing farm operational permits; no discharge or land application changes during study period
- Study does not constitute a lagoon closure, modification, or waste treatment system change requiring state environmental agency approval
